# Soviet Space Dogs Missions Analysis 🐕🚀

In some of my early projects, I focused on **space rocket launches**, but I realized that an important part of early space exploration was often overlooked — **animals used in space experiments**. 

Before human spaceflight became possible, animals were used to test the **effects of microgravity, acceleration, and life-support systems**. Among these efforts, the **Soviet Union** was a pioneer in conducting such biological experiments.

In this project, I created a **dataset and dictionary of Soviet space dogs**, classifying their missions based on the **final outcomes: Survived or Died**. The goal of this analysis is to visualize how different rocket programs contributed to these missions and what outcomes were observed.

# Dataset Structure

The dataset includes the following key attributes:

- **Dog Name** – Name of the space dog  
- **Mission** – Mission or test program  
- **Year** – Year of launch  
- **Rocket** – Rocket used for the mission  
- **Outcome** – Survival status (Survived / Died)

---

# Visualization Used

To understand the mission flow and outcomes, a **Sankey Diagram** was created showing the relationship between: Test Program → Rocket Program → Mission Outcome

This allows us to track how experimental tests evolved into actual rocket programs and what the final outcomes were.

---

# Key Insights from the Sankey Diagram

## 1. Evolution of Rocket Testing Programs

The analysis shows that **R1 and R7 tests have the largest flow**, indicating that most dogs were used in these experimental programs.

From the visualization, a clear transition is visible: R-series Rockets → Sputnik Rockets → Vostok Rockets

- **R-series rockets** were primarily used for **suborbital experiments**.
- These experiments eventually evolved into **Sputnik rockets**, which were responsible for the **first orbital launches**.
- Later developments led to **Vostok rockets**, which became the foundation for **human spaceflight missions**.

This progression reflects how animal experiments helped validate technology before sending humans into space.

---

## 2. Survival vs Mission Failures

The Sankey diagram also shows that **most missions resulted in survival** of the animals, indicating improved safety in later missions.

However, early failures did occur during the experimental phase. One of the most famous examples is:

**Laika**, the first dog to orbit Earth aboard **Sputnik-2**, who unfortunately died due to overheating during the mission.

These early missions were crucial for understanding the challenges of sustaining life in space.

---

## 3. Risk Patterns Across Rocket Programs

The study reveals different survival patterns across rocket programs.

### Higher Survival Programs
- **R7**
- **Vostok**

These rockets show **stronger survival streams**, suggesting improved engineering and mission safety.

### Higher Risk Programs
Higher death rates were observed in:

- **Sputnik missions**
- **Early experimental rocket tests**

These programs were part of the **initial experimental stage**, where technologies were still being tested and refined.

# Importance of the Study

This analysis highlights how **animal missions played a crucial role in early space exploration**. The data demonstrates that:

- Animal experiments helped test **life-support systems**
- They validated **rocket stability and launch survival**
- They paved the way for **human spaceflight missions**

Without these early experiments, missions like **Yuri Gagarin's historic flight in 1961** would not have been possible.
